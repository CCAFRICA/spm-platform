## §1 Problem Statement

Proven from both tenants' real persisted intents (CC DIAG, this session):

- **Meridian c1** (both variants): ratio source `divide(on_time_deliveries, total_deliveries)`, breaks `[85,90,95,98]` (percent-space), `scale={unit:"percent",value:100}`. **Internally coherent** — the scale bridges the 0–1 quotient to percent breaks. Correct today.
- **BCL c1 ejecutivo**: ratio source `divide(net_new_deposits, deposit_target)`, breaks `[0.6,0.8,1,1.3]` (ratio-space), **same** `scale.value=100`. **Internally incoherent** — breaks already in the quotient's space, scale is a lie → 1.03×100=103 ≥ 1.3 → 420 max tier → Oct +21,840 / Nov +20,660 / Dec +15,860 overpay.
- **BCL c1 ejecutivo-senior**: evaluator-side, no scale survives (HF-277 omits) → correct.

Three construction-layer attempts (HF-274 attach, HF-276 pre-multiply, HF-278 omit-both-sides) each fixed one configuration and broke the other, because the determinant — **which space the breaks are in** — is not encoded in the operand shape, and inferring it from magnitudes is a Korean Test violation. HF-278's HALT-2 proved this on real data.

**Root cause:** the recognizer emits break-space inconsistently for structurally identical ratio bands (Meridian percent, BCL ratio), and pairs scale declarations that may or may not agree with the breaks. The breaks node (`BandedLookupDimension`) is a bare `number[]` with no unit; the unit lives in a sibling `scale` object; nothing enforces agreement. The authoritative-space information exists only in the source plan text at recognition time — no downstream layer can recover it.

**The fix (one invariant, two enforcement points, shipped together):**

A DAG-divide band's breaks are declared in the quotient's own space. Therefore no scale accompanies them.

1. **Recognition (where the semantics live):** the prompt instructs that any band whose `reference_source.type==='ratio'` emits its breaks in the ratio's own space (a plan saying "≥85% on-time" emits 0.85; "≥1.3× attainment" emits 1.3) and emits **no scale** for that band. Plus a deterministic loud-failure invariant at recognition output: if a ratio-source dimension arrives with an accompanying scale, structured resolution failure — never silent construction.
2. **Construction (resilience):** `buildConstantWithScale` omits `meta.scale` for any DAG-divide compare operand, **both sides** (generalizing HF-277's evaluator-only rule). With recognition coherent, there is nothing to omit; the omit is the belt to recognition's suspenders.

Neither alone is sufficient: construction-omit alone floors Meridian's current percent-break intent; recognition-change alone leaves HF-274 scaling any stale or non-conforming intent. Together, with cold re-import, both tenants regenerate coherent intents and reconcile.

This is not a registry: one coherence invariant keyed on declared structure (`reference_source.type`), no tenant/component/field names, no magnitude or break-space inference anywhere. Violation = loud structured failure, the AUD-009 shape: general invariant at recognition, loud-failure guard, cause and enforcement together.

## §2 Substrate-Bound Discipline

**Decision 158 (division of labor):** recognition owns semantics — it alone sees "≥85%" in plan text and knows the space. Construction stays deterministic and space-blind. This HF puts the space decision where the information exists and removes the need for construction to guess.

**Decision 154 (Korean Test):** recognition instruction and invariant key on `reference_source.type==='ratio'` and presence/absence of a `scale` node — declared structure only. Construction predicate is `prime==='arithmetic' && op==='divide'`. No string literals for fields/components/tenants, no magnitude thresholds, anywhere. Structured failure on violation per the one-canonical-surface rule.

**AUD-009 (no registry):** one invariant ("quotient-space breaks, no scale, for ratio sources"), not an enumeration of failure shapes. The loud-failure guard rejects the incoherent class wholesale rather than patching instances.

**SR-34 (class):** any ratio-keyed band, any tenant, any side, any domain. BCL and Meridian are the first two instances.

**DD-7:** single pre-computed `reference` operands (not divides) keep their scale path untouched — `ScaleSpec` remains valid and consumed for them. Non-band components untouched. HF-275 untouched.

**Progressive Performance:** intents regenerate on cold re-import; fingerprints re-form on the coherent shape. Verify non-amnesiac behavior on second import.

## §6A Residuals

- **Prompt drift risk:** the recognition instruction is LLM-behavioral; the deterministic loud-failure invariant (2.2) is the permanent guard — a future model revision that re-emits scale+ratio pairings fails loudly at recognition output, never silently miscalculates. This pairing (instruction + invariant) is the durable shape.
- **Plans whose text states thresholds only in percent for a ratio metric:** recognition converts at emission ("85%" ⇒ 0.85). If a future plan's semantics genuinely resist quotient-space expression, the invariant fails loudly and the case surfaces for design — it does not silently construct.
